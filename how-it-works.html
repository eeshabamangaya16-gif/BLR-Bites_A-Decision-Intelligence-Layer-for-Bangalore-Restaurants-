The method

Hype, quality, reliability.

Star ratings alone are misleading. A 3.8 with thousands of reviews is a very different signal from a 4.6 with forty. BLR Bites separates popularity from quality so you can make better choices.

Why ratings alone fail

A 4.8 with 20 reviews and a 4.4 with 8,000 reviews are very different signals.

BLR Bites separates popularity, quality, and confidence so recommendations feel more trustworthy and less driven by noise.

Step 01

Normalize

Popularity measures visibility and public attention. Quality uses Bayesian-adjusted ratings and weighted scaling so tiny sample sizes do not instantly look elite.

Show technical logic
Popularity = log(review_count) ÷ log(max_review_count)
Adjusted Rating = (v / (v + m) × R) + (m / (v + m) × C)
Quality = clamp((weighted rating - 3) ÷ 2, 0, 1)

Step 02

Compare

Hype = Popularity - Quality
Underrated = Quality - Popularity
Reliability reflects confidence derived from review volume.

Hype and Underrated are opposites, while reliability remains independent. A restaurant can be highly reliable yet overhyped, or a hidden gem with lower confidence.

Step 03

Classify

  • Overhyped when visibility significantly exceeds quality while the review base provides strong confidence.
  • Hidden Gem when quality significantly exceeds visibility while confidence remains statistically reliable.
  • Worth It when popularity and quality remain balanced or consistently dependable.

Popularity vs Quality

Points above the diagonal punch above their popularity. Points below it are not necessarily bad restaurants; they are simply more popular than their quality signal suggests.
